1. Use the Right Click the Right Way:
There's no right click on mac, but like on windows, 
the right click gives you a wealth of options
and these are actually several ways to do it.

i. The way apple wants you t do it is by 
 a. Holding Down Ctrl key and Click your mouse/trackpad.
            ------------Important-------------
The right Click(for whatever reason) deactivated by default.
To Enable It.
i. Click on Top Bar's Apple Logo.
ii. Click on 2nd Option called System Settings.
iii. Scroll Down click on "mouse" See 3 options at which click on 
'secondary' click.
Now Magic Mouse will work like any other mouse.
           ----------------X------------------

2. Search for Anything on Mac OS:
Windows Search, which is prominently placed in the taskbar too, 
Has it's Mac equivalent with Spotlight Search.
Whenever you are on your Mac, 
Just press 'cmd' + "Space Bar" Brings Up the "Spotlight Search".
         ----------------IMP---------------------
Your can find basically anything on your mac and even beyond 
- simple starting with apps, settings, files or even text within 
files.
You can visually search your photos look up weather conditions,
type in currency conversions, or do quick calculations.
and all. 
Without leaving your keyboard:
i. Use the arrow keys to move up and down your search results,
ii. Use Space Bar too get a quick preview of the file.
iii. Drag and drop anything out of spotlight search into 
let's say 'email' and it's attached.
And finally, if you've found the file you were looking for 
But want to see where it's located on your Mac,
i. Hit 'cmd' and 'R' to reveal it in the finder.
      -------------------X------------------------

3. Show File Path:
macOS initially, look very confusing and you have 
no clue where you are right now.
i. Let's click "view" at Top bar and select "Show Path Bar".

In case you've missed this feature from the file explorer,
it now shows you exactly where your are in the folder hierarchy.

4. Preview Any File:
You can always hit the space bar to get a sneak peak into 
the file's content without actually opening it in app.
Works with images, videos, audio, files, PDF documents,
so basically any file type.
